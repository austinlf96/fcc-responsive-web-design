Introduction

So you've heard about Tarot, but you're still not sure what exactly it is? Well you've come to the right place. You're not alone in feeling confused or uncertain about what Tarot is or is not. With so many cards, decks, illustrations, layouts, and interpretations it's almost impossible to not get confused! Well this is just the guide you've been looking for! I will break down the various parts of Tarot so you can walk away feeling like you actually understand!

Tarot is a divinitory practice involving a deck cards. Typically this deck is 78 cards broken down into 22 Major Arcana cards and 56 Minor Arcana cards (which I will go into further detail below). Contrary to popular belief, Tarot is not used to predict the future. Tarot is designed to act more like a therapist- a mirror that will echo your thoughts challenging you to discover deep-hidden thoughts, feelings, and beliefs. Ideally you can use this information to improve all areas of your life including love, life, emotions, career, health, and more.

What is Tarot

History of Tarot

Tarot began as a deck for card games in the mid-15th century around various parts of Europe. It was used for various games, many of which are still played today. It wasn't until late in the 18th century that Tarot cards became part a divinitory practice. In 1789 the first Tarot deck designed for divinitory or occult purposes was issued by Jean-Baptiste Alliette. A common belief was that the cards had originated from the book of Thoth, and so the deck centered around themes pertaining to Ancient Egypt. Since then, many interpretations of the Tarot deck have been published, but they typically represent the same meaning with different illustrations. Common deck themes today are the Tarot of Marseilles, the Rider-Waite-Smith tarot deck, and the Thoth tarot deck.

Purpose of Tarot

The purpose of Tarot is to help one take an inward journey to discover parts of themselves hidden deep underneath the surface. However, there are various ways this can be achieved and thus various types of readings to aid this journey.

  • Divination Tarot Reading: This type of reading is intended to gain insight for current and future situations. This typically involves the belief in a higher power to guide the way.
  • Psycholgical Tarot Reading: The importance of tarot symbolism was attached by the first psychologist, Carl Jung. He may have regarded the tarot cards by representing archetypes: situations embedded in the subconscious of all human being or fundamental types of person.
  • Tarot as a mnemonic device: Symbolic study or some schools of occult thought like the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn consider tarot as a mnemonic device or textbook for their teachings. This might be the cause for the world arcana, which is used to convey the meaning of two sections of the tarot deck. It is the plural form of the Latin word Arcanum that means secret or closed.
How Tarot works

It's a common misconception that Tarot cards tell the future. The cards themselves do not tell the future. However, the person seeking guidance will be able to make sense of what each card represents and how that applies to them. Through this insight a person can peer into their own intuition. In a typical reading, the querent will start by shuffling the cards with specific questions in mind. Then the querent will lay the cards in a spread out fashion facedown. Then they choose cards one by one and hands them to the reader until the spread has been completed. As the reader lays down the cards they will receive impressions and intuitive ideas in their subconscious mind to help answer the questions accurately.

How to read

Beginners will typically make use of a reference book to assist with interpreting the meaning of cards as they come up in spreads. The best way for beginners to truly learn the meaning of the cards is to start by feeling the card. Take in its imagry as a whole and pay attention to what feelings the imagry invokes. Do your best to think deeply about what this card could be trying to tell you. Look at it much deeper than the illustration. Do your best to give what's happening a chance to represent something. It is best to write this interpretation down, then reference the classic meaning of the card and take note of this as well. As you continue to pull cards and make your way through the entire deck, you will piece together the story of the Tarot as a whole. The story of you, as a whole.

Customs

There are a few customs in regards to Tarot readings as well as caring for the decks themselves. First, it is customary to prepare an area for doing readings. Some will even perform rituals as part of the prepartion to do a reading. This could include burning incense, altering the lighting, meditating, or whatever else makes you feel more at ease and connected to the ethereal. As for taking cares of your deck, there are a few methods that you can mix and match as you please. Some readers keep their cards wrapped in a silk scarf, in a cloth bag, or inside a box. You can choose to integrate all of these into caring for your cards. It is important to protect your cards from negative energy as well as shield them from physical damage as well.

The Minor Arcana are also called pip cards. There are four suits. Each suit has 10 numbered cards (ace-ten) and 4 court cards (Page, Knight, Queen, King) for a total of 56 cards. Minor Arcana cards represent day to day activies and each suit has a specific association.

  • Cups: Emotions, compassion, feeling, the language of Spirit
  • Swords: Power, action, motivation, conflict, force
  • Wands: Ideas, thoughts, communication, concepts, enlightenment
  • Pentacles: Manifestation, physical creation, physical expression in the world
